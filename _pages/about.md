I am a research scientist holding a Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering from Pennsylvania State University. My research focuses on brain-inspired and neuromorphic computing systems, emphasizing the development and optimization of spatio-temporal neural networks for computer vision and robotics applications, specifically tailored for low-power edge computing platforms.

I also earned an M.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering with a specialization in Control Systems Theory and Algorithms from the University of Calgary, and a B.S. in Electrical and Computer Engineering from Tabriz University.

With a robust interdisciplinary background in artificial intelligence, machine learning, neuromorphic computing, computer vision, robotics, and control systems, I have contributed to both academic and industrial projects, specializing in AI/ML training frameworks and advanced control algorithms. I currently hold the position of Assistant Teaching Professor of Engineering Design at Pennsylvania State University.
